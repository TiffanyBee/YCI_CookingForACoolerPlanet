Cod en Papillote

  • 2 Servings
  • Prep Time: 10 minutes
  • Cook Time: 6 minutes

In French, en papillote means to wrap and cook food in an air-tight, parchment paper package. It is considered a moist-heat cooking method because it is the steam from within the package that cooks the food. In this recipe, cod (or any firm fish) is first covered in strips of vegetables, then enclosed in parchment paper and then cooked in the microwave. Sustainably caught or raised fish is a very climate-friendly choice.

INGREDIENTS

DIRECTIONS

  1. 1Cut 2 pieces of parchment paper, each large enough to enclose a piece of fish.
  2. 2Place a piece of cod in the center of each piece of parchment paper.
  3. 3Sprinkle 1 tbsp of lemon juice over each fish piece.
  4. 4Next place pieces of mushrooms, zucchini, red pepper and green onions on top of each piece.
  5. 5Add a tbsp of butter or Smart Balance and ¼ t tarragon to the top of each fish piece.
  6. 6Fold the parchment paper over the fish, tucking the sides of the paper under each fish piece.
  7. 7Place the fish packets side by side in a microwaveable baking dish.
  8. 8Microwave on high for 6 minutes, rotating after 3 minutes.
  9. 9To serve, carefully unwrap each piece of fish and slide on to each diner’s plate.
